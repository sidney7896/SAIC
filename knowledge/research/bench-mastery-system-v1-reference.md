# Section A: Foundation & Philosophy

## A1. The Three Pillars

This system is built on three fundamental principles that govern all programming decisions:

1. **Consistency Over Intensity** — 20 years of good training beats 5 years of "optimal" training followed by burnout or injury. The best program is the one you can sustain.

2. **Progressive Overload Across All Timescales** — Session to session, month to month, year to year. Small, consistent gains compound into extraordinary results.

3. **Specificity Increases As You Advance** — Beginners need variety to find what works. Elites need specificity to refine what works.

# Section C: Annual Cycle Structure

## C1. The Annual Framework

Every training year follows a consistent structure that feeds into the August 29 peak. This creates predictable, reproducible results year after year.

| Period | Duration | Focus |
|--------|----------|-------|
| September | 4 weeks | PIVOT + REBUILD: Active recovery, introduce new variations, rebuild volume base |
| Oct - Dec | 12 weeks | CYCLE 1: Year-Round Builder (Accumulation → Intensification → Realization) |
| Jan - Mar | 12 weeks | CYCLE 2: Year-Round Builder (continue progression) |
| Apr - Mid Jun | 10-12 weeks | CYCLE 3: Year-Round Builder (final accumulation before peak) |
| Mid Jun - Aug 29 | 10 weeks | PEAKING PROGRAM 1.0: Volume down, intensity up, taper, TEST |

## C2. Phase-Specific Adjustments

### During Cut (Special Case)

| Parameter | Target | Rationale |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Frequency | 3-4x/week | Reduced recovery capacity in deficit |
| Volume | -25-30% from bulk | Muscle retention, not growth |
| Intensity | Maintain (RPE 8-9 singles) | Strength signal preserved |
| Top Single | Weekly | Keeps CNS primed |
| Variations | Minimal | Focus on comp bench |

### During Lean Bulk (Primary Phase)

| Parameter | Target | Rationale |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Frequency | 5-6x/week | Maximum skill acquisition |
| Volume | 18-24 hard sets/week | Hypertrophy + strength |
| Intensity | Undulating (DUP) | Optimal stimulus variation |
| Top Single | Weekly | Track progression |
| Variations | Rotate per block | Address weaknesses |

### During Peak (10 Weeks Before Test)

Switch to Peaking Program 1.0. Key changes:

- Frequency: 3-4x/week (fatigue management)
- Volume: Decreasing (16 → 0 hard sets)
- Intensity: Increasing (RPE 7.5 → 9+)
- Variations: Only high-transfer (Spoto, paused, close-grip paused)

# Section G: Progression & Autoregulation

## G1. Load Progression Rules

### Weekly: Top Single Progression

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Top single RPE < target | +2.5 kg next week |
| Top single RPE = target | Maintain weight |
| Top single RPE > target | −2.5 kg OR take extra rest before next heavy day |
| Technical breakdown | Stop session, log issue, assess recovery |

### Weekly: Rep Progression (Medium Days)

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| All sets below target RPE | +1 rep per set next week (up to rep ceiling) |
| Rep ceiling reached | +2.5 kg, reset to lower rep range |

## G2. Autoregulation Override Rules

- RPE 9.5+ on any work set → Stop the set immediately, note in log
- 2 sessions in a row above target RPE → Mini-deload (−20% volume for 3 days)
- Any grinding rep in training → Session complete, do not continue

## G3. E1RM Calculation

Use the RPE-to-%1RM relationship to estimate your true max from any single:

| Single @ RPE | ≈ % of 1RM | E1RM Formula |
|--------------|------------|--------------|
| 7 | 89% | e1RM = weight ÷ 0.89 |
| 7.5 | 90.5% | e1RM = weight ÷ 0.905 |
| 8 | 92% | e1RM = weight ÷ 0.92 |
| 8.5 | 94% | e1RM = weight ÷ 0.94 |
| 9 | 96% | e1RM = weight ÷ 0.96 |
| 9.5 | 98% | e1RM = weight ÷ 0.98 |

**Example:** 92.5 kg @ RPE 8 → e1RM = 92.5 ÷ 0.92 = 100.5 kg

## G4. Back-Off Set Calculations

After establishing your e1RM for the day, calculate back-off weights:

| Block | Back-off % | Example (e1RM=100) | Purpose |
|-------|------------|-------------------|---------|
| Accumulation | 74-79% | 74-79 kg | Volume building |
| Intensification | 80-86% | 80-86 kg | Strength conversion |
| Realization | 86-90% | 86-90 kg | Peak expression |

# Section H: Recovery & Deload Protocols

## H1. Recovery Priorities

### Sleep (Non-Negotiable)

| Parameter | Target | Why It Matters |
|-----------|--------|----------------|
| Duration | 7.5-9 hours | Muscle protein synthesis peaks during sleep |
| Consistency | Same time ±30 min | Circadian rhythm optimization |
| Quality | Dark, cool, quiet | Deep sleep is where gains happen |

### Nutrition (Phase-Dependent)

| Phase | Calories | Protein | Weight Change |
|-------|----------|---------|---------------|
| Cut | −500 kcal deficit | 2.0-2.4 g/kg | −0.5 kg/week |
| Lean Bulk | +300-500 kcal surplus | 1.6-2.2 g/kg | +0.25-0.5 kg/week |
| Peak | Maintenance | 1.8-2.0 g/kg | Stable |

## H2. Deload Triggers (2 of 3 = Deload)

1. **E1RM drops ≥2%** over 2 comparable sessions
2. **RPE drift** — same weight feels harder than previous weeks
3. **Pain trending up** — average pain score ≥3/10

## H3. Deload Protocol

| Parameter | Adjustment |
|-----------|------------|
| Volume | −40% (reduce sets, not reps) |
| Intensity | RPE −1 across all sessions |
| Frequency | MAINTAIN (skill retention is crucial) |
| Duration | 1 week (extend to 2 if still fatigued) |
| Accessories | Cut in half or eliminate |

## H4. Mini-Deload (Emergency Protocol)

Use when you need quick recovery but a full deload week is not warranted:

- Duration: 3 days
- Volume: −20%
- Intensity: RPE −0.5
- Trigger: 2 consecutive sessions above target RPE

# Section K: Injury Prevention & Pain Management

## K1. Pain Response Protocol

| Pain Level | Response |
|------------|----------|
| 0-2/10 | Train as planned. Log and monitor. |
| 3-4/10 | Reduce bench volume by 33%. RPE −0.5 on heavy days. Switch to joint-friendly variants. |
| 5+/10 | STOP the exercise immediately. Pivot to pain-free movements for 7-10 days. Check red flags. |
| Sharp pain | STOP. Ice. Rest 48 hours. If persists, seek professional help. |
| Pop/tear | STOP immediately. Do not test. Seek medical attention same day. |

## K2. Red Flags (Immediate Attention Required)

- Pop or tearing sensation with hematoma (chest/armpit)
- Sharp pain ≥5/10 during any rep
- New tingling, numbness, or weakness in arms/hands
- Pain trending ≥4/10 week over week
- Joint instability or giving way

## K3. Common Problem Areas & Prevention

### Shoulders

- **Prevention:** Daily band pull-aparts (100 reps), external rotations 3x/week
- **Warning signs:** Anterior shoulder pain, clicking, weakness in external rotation
- **Response:** Narrow grip slightly, reduce incline work, increase rear delt volume

### Elbows

- **Prevention:** Wrist curls/extensions 2x/week, avoid excessive tricep isolation
- **Warning signs:** Inner elbow pain (golfer's), outer elbow pain (tennis)
- **Response:** Reduce close-grip work, switch to hammer curls, use elbow sleeves

### Pecs

- **Prevention:** Proper warm-up, no excessive stretch at bottom, control descent
- **Warning signs:** Chest tightness, cramping, sharp pain near armpit
- **Response:** Reduce ROM slightly, no wide-grip work, pause longer at bottom

## K4. Warm-Up Protocol

Complete this before every bench session:

| Order | Exercise | Volume | Purpose |
|-------|----------|--------|---------|
| 1 | General cardio | 5 min | Raise body temp |
| 2 | Band pull-aparts | 25 reps | Shoulder activation |
| 3 | External rotations | 10 each arm | Rotator cuff |
| 4 | Push-ups | 10-15 reps | Pec activation |
| 5 | Empty bar bench | 2×10 | Movement pattern |
| 6 | Graduated warm-up sets | 40%, 60%, 75%, 85% | Build to working weight |

# Section L: The Non-Negotiables

These rules are absolute. Breaking them consistently will derail progress or cause injury.

## L1. The 15 Commandments

1. **Competition bench ≥2x/week year-round.** Skill maintenance requires frequency.

2. **In the last 10 weeks before test: comp bench ≥3x/week.**

3. **Volume is the primary knob.** Deload/taper = reduce sets, maintain intensity.

4. **No structural grinders outside test day.** Save maximal effort for when it counts.

5. **Top single is always technically clean.** If form breaks down, stop the session.

6. **Deload at 2-of-3 triggers.** E1RM drop, RPE drift, or pain increase.

7. **Pivot 2 weeks after August 29 is mandatory.** Mental and physical recovery after peak.

8. **Pain ≥5/10 or any "pop" = STOP + protocol.**

9. **Variations change only at deload/pivot.** No random exercise switching mid-block.

10. **Log minimum: top single, e1RM, hard sets, pain, sleep.**

11. **Sleep ≥7 hours is non-negotiable.** Gains happen during recovery.

12. **Protein ≥1.6g/kg daily.** Higher during cut (2.0-2.4g/kg).

13. **Warm up fully every session.** No exceptions.

14. **Document PR attempts on video.** Build the evidence trail.

15. **Annual review on August 29 is sacred.** Test, reflect, adjust for next year.

# Section M: Natty Documentation Strategy

At 260-300kg, skepticism is inevitable. This strategy creates an irrefutable evidence trail.

## M1. Documentation Tiers

### Tier 1: Essential (Start Now)

| What | Frequency | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|
| PR videos (all 3 variants) | Annually (Aug 29) | Shows progression curve over 15+ years |
| Complete training log | Every session | Impossible to fake over years |
| Physique photos | Every 3-6 months | Shows gradual change, no "overnight" transformation |
| Bodyweight log | Weekly | Traceable progression |

### Tier 2: Strong Evidence

| What | Why It Matters |
|------|----------------|
| Annual bloodwork | Normal testosterone, LH, FSH, hematocrit. Not proof, but absence of suspicious markers. |
| DEXA scans | Shows realistic muscle gain rate (~1-2kg/year after beginner phase) |
| "Bad day" videos | Not just PRs — sessions where you fail or struggle. Shows authenticity. |
| Unedited gym footage | Long clips without cuts, visible fatigue, realistic gym environment. |

### Tier 3: Maximum Credibility

| What | Why It Matters |
|------|----------------|
| Full session time-lapses | Proves you don't just "film the PR" |
| Setback documentation | Injuries, stagnation, deloads — PED users rarely show this |
| Consistent gym presence | People can witness your progression in real-time |
| Proportional other lifts | If squat/deadlift are proportional, more believable |
| Community reputation | Respected natural lifters vouching for you |

## M2. The Smoking Gun: Your Progression Curve

The most convincing evidence is the shape of your progression over time. A natural curve looks like:

- Rapid gains years 1-3
- Noticeable slowdown years 4-6
- Minimal gains years 7+
- NO sudden jumps of +20kg in 6 months

If your documented curve follows this pattern over 15-20 years, it's the strongest possible evidence.

## M3. Practical Documentation Schedule

| When | Action |
|------|--------|
| Daily | Log session in app/spreadsheet (weight, sets, RPE, pain) |
