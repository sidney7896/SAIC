## 2. Current Phase: Cut (February 2026 → ~Late March 2026)

### 2.1 Situation

- Bodyweight dropping from ~71.8 kg → 65–67.5 kg at ~1 kg/week.
- Recovery is reduced due to caloric deficit.
- Goal: preserve bench strength (maintain ~95 kg, do not drop below 90 kg).
- Bench is still performed every session (push, pull, and leg days).
- No formal program — auto-regulated.

### 2.2 Cut Bench Rules

| Parameter | Prescription |
|-----------|-------------|
| Frequency | Heavy on push days, medium on leg days. Pull days: minimal or skipped (easy sets at ~60 kg if anything). |
| Volume | Reduced vs. bulk. ~75% of peak volume. |
| Intensity | Maintain. Weekly heavy single **target**: ~1.5–2.5 RiR (≈ RPE 7.5–8.5). No grinders (RPE 10). |
| Safety override | If the top single ends up **≤1 RiR** (≈ RPE ≥9): keep it if technique is clean, but reduce back-off volume **~20–33%** that day (fatigue management). |
| Rep range | Primarily 1–5 reps. Paused variants, heavy singles, back-offs. |
| Variations | Comp bench (paused + touch-and-go), close-grip, Spoto press. Keep it specific. |
| Rest | 3–5 minutes for heavy work. |

### 2.3 Push Day Bench Protocol (Heavy Days)

| Component | Prescription | Target Intensity | Rest |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|------|
| Warm-up pyramid | Bar × 10, ~40% × 8, ~60% × 5, ~75% × 2–3 | — | 1:00–2:00 |
| Top single (comp bench, paused) | 1 × 1 | Target: ~1.5–2.5 RiR (RPE 7.5–8.5), auto-regulated | 5:00 |
| Back-off sets | 3–4 × 3–5 reps | 1.5–2.5 RiR | 3:00–4:00 |
| Optional: paused variant or close-grip | 2–3 × 3–5 reps | 2–2.5 RiR | 3:00 |

### 2.4 Leg Day Bench Protocol (Medium Days)

| Component | Prescription | Target Intensity | Rest |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|------|
| Warm-up | Bar × 10, ~50% × 5–6 | — | 1:00 |
| Working sets | 3–5 × 3–5 reps (touch-and-go or light paused) | 3–4 RiR (RPE 6–7) | 2:00–3:00 |

Purpose: volume accumulation, variation work, movement pattern practice.

### 2.5 Pull Day Bench (Minimal / Optional)

- Not programmed. If the athlete benches on pull days, it is very light — easy sets at ~60 kg or whatever requires zero difficulty.
- The LLM should not prescribe bench work on pull days unless the athlete requests it.

### 2.6 Acceptable Strength Loss During Cut

| Threshold | Interpretation | Action |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| e1RM stable or ↑ | Excellent — strength maintained or gained despite deficit | Continue as planned |
| e1RM drop ≤3% | Acceptable | Monitor, no changes needed |
| e1RM drop 3–5% + RPE drift | Warning | Reduce volume by 20%, consider deload |
| e1RM drop >5% | Red flag | Deload week, reassess caloric deficit or training stress |

### 2.7 Comparable Session Rule (for Trend Decisions)

To avoid false alarms, compare only **comparable sessions** when evaluating e1RM trend and deload triggers:

1. Compare push-day heavy sessions to push-day heavy sessions.
2. Compare leg-day medium sessions to leg-day medium sessions.
3. Exclude pull-day optional/light bench from trigger calculations unless no other data exists.
4. If data is mixed, use the latest two comparable points and mark confidence as reduced.

This rule applies to:
- e1RM drop trigger (>=2%)
- RPE drift trigger (>=1 RPE at similar load)
- Safety override frequency trend

## 5. Key Reference: e1RM Calculation

Use the RPE-to-%1RM relationship to estimate true max from any single:

| Single @ RPE | ≈ % of 1RM | e1RM Formula |
|--------------|------------|--------------|
| 6 | 86% | e1RM = weight ÷ 0.86 |
| 6.5 | 87.5% | e1RM = weight ÷ 0.875 |
| 7 | 89% | e1RM = weight ÷ 0.89 |
| 7.5 | 90.5% | e1RM = weight ÷ 0.905 |
| 8 | 92% | e1RM = weight ÷ 0.92 |
| 8.5 | 94% | e1RM = weight ÷ 0.94 |
| 9 | 96% | e1RM = weight ÷ 0.96 |
| 9.5 | 98% | e1RM = weight ÷ 0.98 |

**Example:** 95 kg @ RPE 8 → e1RM = 95 ÷ 0.92 = **103.3 kg**

> Note: These are standard RTS/SBS estimates. As the athlete accumulates more test data over years, personalised calibration should replace these defaults.

### 5.1 e1RM Method Selection Rule

Use this decision order:

1. **Single with known RiR/RPE** -> use RPE table method (preferred for bench singles).
2. **Rep set with unknown RPE but valid reps** -> use Epley estimate as fallback.
3. **No usable intensity data** -> do not fabricate e1RM; mark as unavailable for that set.

When both single-based and rep-based estimates are present in one session, report the single-based estimate as primary and rep-based as context only.

## 6. Key Reference: RPE & RiR Conversion

| RPE | RiR | Meaning |
|-----|-----|---------|
| 6 | 4 | Could do 4 more reps. Speed work, light days. |
| 7 | 3 | Could do 3 more reps. Volume work, deload. |
| 8 | 2 | Could do 2 more reps. Standard working sets. |
| 9 | 1 | Could do 1 more rep. Heavy singles, intensification. |
| 10 | 0 | Absolute max. TEST DAY ONLY. |

> Note: The athlete logs RiR in Strengthlog. RiR 2.5 ≈ RPE 7.5, RiR 1.5 ≈ RPE 8.5, RiR 0.5 ≈ RPE 9.5.

## 7. Post-Japan: Bench Mastery System — Year-Round Builder

> **Status:** Not active. Begins after Japan vacation (~May 2026).

This section outlines the year-round bench programming system that will run from post-Japan onward, structured around the annual peak on August 29.

### 7.1 Three Pillars

1. **Consistency Over Intensity** — 20 years of good training beats 5 years of "optimal" training followed by burnout or injury.
2. **Progressive Overload Across All Timescales** — Session to session, month to month, year to year.
3. **Specificity Increases As You Advance** — Beginners need variety; elites need specificity.

### 7.2 Annual Cycle Structure

| Period | Duration | Focus |
|--------|----------|-------|
| September | 4 weeks | PIVOT + REBUILD: Active recovery, introduce new variations, rebuild volume base |
| Oct – Dec | 12 weeks | CYCLE 1: Year-Round Builder (Accumulation → Intensification → Realization) |
| Jan – Mar | 12 weeks | CYCLE 2: Year-Round Builder (continue progression) |
| Apr – Mid Jun | 10–12 weeks | CYCLE 3: Year-Round Builder (final accumulation before peak) |
| Mid Jun – Aug 29 | 10 weeks | PEAKING PROGRAM 1.0 (see Section 8) |

### 7.3 Weekly Structure (6x/week bench, integrated with PPL)

| Day | PPL | Bench Type | Exercise | Sets × Reps | RPE | Rest |
|-----|-----|-----------|----------|-------------|-----|------|
| 1 | Push A | HEAVY | Comp bench (paused) + back-offs | 1×1 + 4×3 | 8.5 / 8 | 5:00 / 4:00 |
| 2 | Pull A | LIGHT | Comp bench (TnG) | 5×5 | 6.5 | 2:30 |
| 3 | Legs A | MEDIUM | Close-grip bench | 4×6 | 7.5 | 3:00 |
| 4 | Push B | HEAVY | Paused bench (variant) | 5×4 | 8 | 4:00 |
| 5 | Pull B | LIGHT | Comp bench (speed) | 6×2 | 6 | 2:00 |
| 6 | Legs B | MEDIUM | Spoto press | 4×5 | 7.5 | 3:00 |
| 7 | Rest | — | Full recovery | — | — | — |

### 7.4 Weekly Volume Targets

| Parameter | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Hard sets (@RPE ≥7) | 17–21 (varies by mesocycle week) |
| Total bench sets | 24–30 |
| Total reps | 80–120 in accumulation, 50–80 in intensification |

### 7.5 Intensity Distribution

| Type | Frequency | RPE Range | Purpose |
|------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| HEAVY | 2x/week | 8–9 | Primary strength stimulus |
| MEDIUM | 2x/week | 7–7.5 | Volume accumulation, hypertrophy |
| LIGHT | 2x/week | 6–6.5 | Skill maintenance, active recovery |

### 7.6 Mesocycle Structure (12-Week Blocks)

Each 12-week cycle contains three 4-week blocks:

| Block | Weeks | Focus | Hard Sets/Week | Top Single RPE |
|-------|-------|-------|---------------|----------------|
| A: Accumulation | 1–4 | High volume, moderate intensity | 17 → 19 → 21 → 10 (deload) | 8.0 → 8.5 |
| B: Intensification | 5–8 | Moderate volume, high intensity | 17 → 17 → 17 → 10 (deload) | 8.5 → 9.0 |
| C: Realization | 9–12 | Lower volume, peak intensity, test e1RM | 14 → 8 → 14 → transition | 8.5 → 9.0–9.5 |

### 7.7 Exercise Rotation by Block

| Block | Day 1 (Heavy) | Day 3 (Medium) | Day 4 (Heavy) | Day 6 (Medium) |
|-------|---------------|----------------|---------------|----------------|
| A | Comp paused | Close-grip | Paused bench | Spoto |
| B | Comp paused | Feet-up bench | Larsen press | CG paused |
| C | Comp paused | Close-grip | Comp paused | Spoto |

Light days (2 & 5) always use competition bench for maximum specificity.

### 7.8 Load Progression Rules

**Top Single:**

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Top single RPE < target | +2.5 kg next week |
| Top single RPE = target | Maintain weight |
| Top single RPE > target | −2.5 kg OR extra rest before next heavy day |
| Technical breakdown | Stop session, log issue, assess recovery |

**Rep Work (Medium Days):**

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| All sets below target RPE | +1 rep per set next week (up to rep ceiling) |
| Rep ceiling reached | +2.5 kg, reset to lower rep range |

### 7.9 Back-Off Set Calculations

| Block | Back-off % of e1RM | Example (e1RM = 100 kg) |
|-------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Accumulation | 74–79% | 74–79 kg |
| Intensification | 80–86% | 80–86 kg |
| Realization | 86–90% | 86–90 kg |

### 7.10 Expected Annual Progress

| Training Age | Expected 1RM Gain/Year |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Years 3–5 | +10–15 kg |
| Years 6–8 | +5–10 kg |
| Years 9–12 | +2.5–5 kg |
| Years 13+ | +0–2.5 kg |

### 7.11 Deload Protocol (within Bench Mastery System)

**Triggers (2 of 3 = deload):**

1. e1RM drops ≥2% over 2 comparable sessions.
2. RPE drift — same weight feels ≥1 RPE harder in 2 sessions.
3. Pain trending up — average ≥3/10.

**Execution:**

| Parameter | Adjustment |
|-----------|------------|
| Volume | −40% (reduce sets, not reps) |
| Intensity | RPE −1 across all sessions |
| Frequency | MAINTAIN (skill retention) |
| Duration | 1 week (extend to 2 if still fatigued) |
| Accessories | Cut in half or eliminate |

**Mini-deload (emergency):** 3 days, −20% volume, RPE −0.5. Use when 2 consecutive sessions exceed target RPE.

**Planned deloads per year:** 4× (end Nov, end Feb, end May, mid Jul if needed) + post-test pivot.

### 7.12 Multi-Year Training Age Phases

| Phase | Years | Frequency | Volume | Variations | Focus |
|-------|-------|-----------|--------|------------|-------|
| Foundation Building | 3–5 | Build to 6x/week | Higher | More variety | Build mass, refine technique |
| Strength Maximization | 6–10 | 5–6x/week | Moderate-high | Narrow to proven exercises | Convert mass to strength |
| Peak Performance | 11–15 | 4–6x/week | Individualised | Minimal, specific | Express accumulated strength |
| Maintenance | 16–20+ | 4–5x/week | Recovery-based | What keeps you healthy | Longevity, occasional PRs |

## 8. Annual Peaking: Peaking Program 1.0 (10 Weeks Before August 29)

> **Status:** Not active. Used annually in the 10 weeks leading to August 29.

### 8.1 Template Length Selection

**Default: 10 weeks.** Choose 8 or 6 only if specific conditions are met:

| Length | When to Choose |
|--------|---------------|
| 10 weeks | Default. Bench 3–4x/week, good logging, technique to optimise. |
| 8 weeks | Already "in shape": ≥3 bench sessions/week, ≥10 hard sets/week, e1RM stable/rising, pain ≤2/10 for last 4 weeks. |
| 6 weeks | Can't execute 8–10 weeks due to cut/stress/schedule, or already high-frequency year-round and just need to polish. |

### 8.2 Microcycle Session Roles

| Session | Role | Description |
|---------|------|-------------|
| A | Heavy Specific (anchor) | Comp bench top single + back-offs |
| B | Volume Builder | Close-grip or touch-and-go (phase-dependent) |
| C | Secondary Intensity | Paused / Spoto — heavy but technically strict |
| D | Low-stress technique/speed (optional) | Low fatigue, high skill quality |

### 8.3 10-Week Peaking Table

| Week | Hard Sets @RPE7+ | Top Single RPE | Key Focus |
|------|-----------------|----------------|-----------|
| W10 | 16 | 7.5 | Volume base, technical "perfect rep" |
| W9 | 16 | 8.0 | Building intensity |
| W8 | 14 | 8.0 | Introduce Spoto, maintain back-offs |
| W7 | 15 | 8.5 | Close-grip paused, heavy doubles |
| W6 | 14 | 8.5 | Intensity up, volume steady |
| W5 | 13 | 9.0 | Clean single — stop at grind. Session D dropped. |
| W4 | 12 | 9.0 | Last "heavy but clean." Commands on all comp bench. |
| W3 | 10 | 8.5 | "Peak exposure" — last real heavy week |
| W2 | 3 | 8.0 | Opener check (T-6). Taper begins. |
| W1 | 0 | 5.5 | Last light technique. Then full rest T-1. |

### 8.4 Allowed Variations During Peak

- ✅ Comp bench (paused + commands), paused bench, Spoto, close-grip (preferably paused), controlled touch-and-go.
- ❌ No heavy dips, heavy OHP, new grips/setups, extreme ROM partials from W6 onward.

### 8.5 Taper Protocol (Final 14 Days)

| Day | What | Prescription |
|-----|------|-------------|
| T-6 (Sun Aug 23, 2026) | Opener check | 1×1 comp bench @ RPE 8.0 |
| T-4 (Tue Aug 25) | Light volume | 3×3 @ 72% e1RM, paused + commands, RPE 6.5 |
| T-2 (Thu Aug 27) | Last movement | 6×1 @ 65% e1RM, RPE 6.0, perfect |
| T-1 (Fri Aug 28) | Full rest | Nothing |
| T-0 (Sat Aug 29) | TEST DAY | See Section 9 |

### 8.6 Autoregulation During Peak

**If RPE runs higher than planned (fatigue day):**
1. Adjust single load down 2.5%.
2. Halve back-off sets.
3. Drop Session D.
4. Drop pressing accessories (keep upper back pulling).

**If you feel "flat" during taper:**
- Add a primer on T-3: 2×1 @ 82% e1RM, RPE 6.5. Then resume taper.

**If pain develops mid-peak:** Follow pain escalation protocol (Section 10).

## 9. Test Day Protocol (August 29)

### 9.1 Testing Order

1. **IPF Competition Standard** (primary — fris, paused, commands, hips down)
2. **Gym No Wraps** (touch-and-go, light bounce, hips down)
3. **Gym + Wraps** (TnG, moderate bounce, wraps, belt, hips may rise)

Rest between standards: 25 minutes.

### 9.2 Warm-Up Protocol (IPF)

| Order | Weight | Reps | Rest After |
|-------|--------|------|-----------|
| 1 | Empty bar | 10 + 5 (1-sec pause) | 1:00 |
| 2 | 40% | 5 | 2:00 |
| 3 | 55% | 3 | 2:30 |
| 4 | 65% | 2 | 3:00 |
| 5 | 75% | 1 | 3:30 |
| 6 | 85% | 1 | 4:00 |
| 7 | 92% | 1 | 5:00 |
| 8 | Opener | — | — |

### 9.3 Attempt Selection (IPF)

| Attempt | Weight | Target RPE |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| Opener | 92% of expected IPF 1RM | 7.5–8.0 |
| 2nd | 97% if opener felt normal; 95% if RPE 8.5; 93% if RPE ≥9.0 | — |
| 3rd | +2.5 kg if 2nd RPE ≤9.0; repeat/+1.25 kg if RPE 9.5; stop if RPE 10 | — |

### 9.4 No-Wrap & Wraps Attempts (Fatigue-Aware)

- Max 2 attempts per standard.
- Opener: 95% of expected 1RM for that standard @ RPE 8.
- 2nd: +2.5 kg (no wraps) or +5 kg (wraps) if opener was fast. Otherwise +0.
- 3rd attempt only if 2nd was RPE ≤9.0 and technically clean.

### 9.5 Failed Lift Protocol

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| IPF opener fails | 5–7 min rest, repeat exact weight with 1 cue fix |
| IPF 2nd fails (technique) | Repeat with 1 cue fix after 7 min |
| IPF 2nd fails (pure strength) | Lower 3rd to 97% of 2nd |
| 2 misses in a row on same standard | Stop that standard, move to next |
| Pain ≥5/10 or technique "collapse" | Stop all further max attempts |

### 9.6 Three Testing Variants — Expected Conversion

| Variant | Typical Boost vs IPF |
|---------|---------------------|
| IPF (Comp Standard) | Baseline |
| Gym (No Wraps) | +3–7% |
| Gym + Wraps | +12–18% |

> These factors will be personalised as the athlete accumulates annual test data.

## 10. Injury Prevention & Pain Management (Bench-Specific)

### 10.1 Pain Escalation Protocol

| Level | Pain | Response |
|-------|------|----------|
| 1 | 0–2/10 | Train as planned. Log before/after/next morning. Adjust grip if needed. |
| 2 | 3–4/10 (no function loss) | Bench variant volume −33%. Top single target RPE −0.5. B-slot → joint-friendly variant. |
| 3 | ≥5/10 or function loss | STOP that exercise. 7–10 day pivot (RPE cap 7, comp bench light or out). |
| 4 | Acute (pop/swelling/instability) | STOP. Seek medical attention same day. |

### 10.2 Red Flags (Stop Immediately)

- Pop or tearing sensation with hematoma (chest/armpit).
- Sharp pain ≥5/10 during any rep.
- New tingling, numbness, or weakness in arms/hands.
- Pain trending ≥4/10 week over week.
- Joint instability or giving way.

### 10.3 Common Problem Areas

| Area | Warning Signs | Prevention | Response |
|------|--------------|------------|----------|
| Shoulders | Anterior pain, clicking, external rotation weakness | Daily band pull-aparts, external rotations 3x/week | Narrow grip, reduce incline, increase rear delt volume |
| Elbows | Inner pain (golfer's), outer pain (tennis) | Wrist curls/extensions 2x/week | Reduce close-grip, switch to hammer curls, elbow sleeves |
| Pecs | Tightness, cramping, sharp pain near armpit | Proper warm-up, controlled descent, no excessive stretch | Reduce ROM slightly, no wide grip, longer pauses |

### 10.4 Accessory Minimums for Bench Longevity (Year-Round)

| Exercise | Purpose | Frequency | Volume | In Peak |
|----------|---------|-----------|--------|---------|
| Rear delts | Shoulder balance | 2x/week | 3×15 @ RPE 8 | 2×15 |
| Rotator cuff / scap control | Stability, irritation prevention | 3x/week | 2×20 @ RPE 7 | Maintain (light) |
| Upper back | Bench setup, bar path, shoulder comfort | 3x/week | 3×10 @ RPE 8 | 2×10 |
| Triceps (elbow-friendly) | Lockout capacity | 2x/week | 3×12 @ RPE 8 | 2×12 |
| Wrist/forearm | Wrist stability at high frequency | 2x/week | 2×15 ext + 2×15 pron/sup | 1×15 |
| Serratus/scap push-ups (optional) | Scapular stability | 2x/week | 2×15 @ RPE 6–7 | Optional |

### 10.5 Warm-Up Protocol (Every Bench Session)

| Order | Exercise | Volume | Purpose |
|-------|----------|--------|---------|
| 1 | General cardio | 5 min | Raise body temp |
| 2 | Band pull-aparts | 25 reps | Shoulder activation |
| 3 | External rotations | 10 each arm | Rotator cuff |
| 4 | Push-ups | 10–15 reps | Pec activation |
| 5 | Empty bar bench | 2×10 | Movement pattern |
| 6 | Graduated warm-up sets | 40%, 60%, 75%, 85% | Build to working weight |

## 11. Non-Negotiables (Bench-Specific)

1. Competition bench ≥2x/week year-round.
2. In the last 10 weeks before test: comp bench ≥3x/week.
3. Volume is the primary knob (deload/taper = reduce sets, maintain intensity).
4. No structural grinders outside test day.
5. Top single is always technically clean — if form breaks, stop.
6. Deload at 2-of-3 triggers (e1RM↓, RPE drift, pain↑).
7. Pivot 2 weeks after August 29 is mandatory.
8. Pain ≥5/10 or any "pop" = STOP + protocol.
9. Variations change only at deload/pivot, not randomly mid-block.
10. Log minimum: top single, e1RM, hard sets, pain, sleep.
11. Sleep ≥7 hours is non-negotiable.
12. Protein ≥1.6 g/kg daily (higher during cut: 2.0–2.4 g/kg).
13. Warm up fully every session.
14. Document PR attempts on video.
15. Annual review on August 29 is sacred — test, reflect, adjust.
